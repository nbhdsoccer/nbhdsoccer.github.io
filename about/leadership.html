Organizational Governance & Transparency

Our commitment to transparent, accountable nonprofit operations that serve our community's authentic needs

Legal Structure

Illinois Not-For-Profit Corporation with EIN. 501(c)(3) federal tax-exempt status pending with IRS. All governance documents available for community review.

Volunteer-Powered Model

100% volunteer-powered organization. All leadership positions are unpaid community members passionate about neighborhood development. No executive salaries or administrative overhead.

Financial Transparency

Transparent community investment model with all financial decisions made by board of directors and shared with community. Annual financial reports available to all members.

Community Accountability

Regular community meetings, open communication channels, and commitment to neighbor-led decision making. Community feedback directly shapes our programming and priorities.

Conflict of Interest Policy

Clear policies ensuring board decisions prioritize community benefit over personal gain. All potential conflicts disclosed publicly and managed through transparent processes.

Democratic Leadership

Consensus-based decision making for major initiatives. Community input actively sought for program development, partnership decisions, and organizational direction.

Why We're All Volunteers

"We believe the best community development comes from neighbors who care about their neighborhoods, not people collecting paychecks. Every hour our leadership team donates goes directly to strengthening our communities because this work matters to us personally - we live here, our families are here, and we're building something for all of us together."

This isn't just about keeping costs low - it's about authentic community leadership. When neighbors lead neighbors, the work stays true to what our communities actually need.
